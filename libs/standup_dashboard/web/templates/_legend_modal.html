Ack & Total scale with the number of regions selected; the resolve rate and the MTTR/MTTA means do not (they're rates, not volumes).

  • Alert MTTR & MTTA in the pulse-history table are coloured vs the previous pulse, not by the fixed thresholds above: at or below the healthy floor (MTTR 30m, MTTA 5m), or faster than the previous pulse · within ±10% of the previous pulse · slower than the previous pulse. (The counts table keeps the fixed thresholds.)
  • New columns are coloured as intake vs the previous pulse — fewer new tickets than last pulse is , more is , about the same . New Total additionally shows whenever it's at or below the historical average intake (a healthy load), regardless of the change.
  • ISReq Days to Close is mean weekdays from created to done (weekends excluded), coloured: we closed more than we received this pulse, or faster than the previous pulse · within ±10% of the previous pulse · slower than the previous pulse. (Closing a backlog of old tickets raises the average, so out-closing intake stays green.)
  • Closed PR/MP is coloured by keep-up vs New PR/MP Review: closed ≥ requested · one left behind · two or more behind.
  • To Do / WIP / Success come from the Jira status; Distractors are tickets reclassified as off-task for the role.
  • Success (Done) is green for everyone — except a Project engineer's non-ISDB work, which stays a red distractor even when done.
  • Non-assigned touches: green for PVG/BVG, red for GEN/Project/OFF.
  • Alerts are coloured by the handler's role (table above), regardless of acked/resolved. In the panel they still group as resolved → Success, open → WIP, and GEN's alerts → Distractors. A PVG ticket in In Review is a yellow distractor.
  • Weekend has no role — only the on-call engineer is active; everyone else is OFF (all red).
